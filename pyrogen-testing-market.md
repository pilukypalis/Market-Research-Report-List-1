<p><p>The pyrogen testing market encompasses various methodologies for detecting pyrogens, primarily endotoxins. The LAL test utilizes Limulus amebocyte lysate for sensitivity. Chromogenic tests provide quantitative results through colorimetric changes. Turbidimetric tests measure light scattering to detect endotoxin presence. Gel clot tests yield results based on gel formation in response to endotoxins. In vitro pyrogen tests analyze immune responses without live animals, while rabbit tests involve live subjects to assess fever response. Each method caters to specific regulatory and sensitivity needs.</p></p>
