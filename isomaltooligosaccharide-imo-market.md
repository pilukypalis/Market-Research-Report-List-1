<p><p>Isomaltooligosaccharides (IMOs) are non-digestible oligosaccharides derived from starch, primarily used as a low-calorie sweetener and dietary fiber. The market for IMOs encompasses various types, including isomaltose, panose, and isomalt trisaccharides. Isomaltose consists of two glucose units and is valued for its low glycemic index. Panose, a trisaccharide, offers sweetness and functionality in food products. Isomalt trisaccharides provide texture and stability in formulations. These variants cater to diverse applications in food, beverages, and nutraceuticals, driven by consumer demand for healthier alternatives.</p></p>
