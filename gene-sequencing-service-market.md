<p><p>The gene sequencing service market comprises several types based on technological advancements. First-generation sequencing, known for its Sanger method, provides high accuracy but limited throughput. Second-generation sequencing, or next-generation sequencing (NGS), enables massive parallel sequencing, increasing speed and reducing costs significantly. Third-generation sequencing offers real-time, single-molecule sequencing, allowing for longer reads and improved accuracy. Fourth-generation sequencing focuses on ultra-rapid sequencing for real-time applications, pushing the boundaries of genomics by enhancing accessibility and efficiency in various research and clinical settings.</p></p>
