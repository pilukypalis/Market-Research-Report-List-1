<p><p>Flatwire and hairpin motor markets encompass various winding types that influence motor performance and efficiency. Hairpin winding utilizes U-shaped wires for compact designs, while rectangular winding employs flat wires for improved thermal management. Distributed winding enhances torque and reduces harmonics through spread coils, while concentrated winding focuses on efficiency by grouping coils closely. Wave winding, characterized by its overlap and interconnection, optimizes magnetic field utilization. Each type offers unique advantages for applications in electric vehicles, robotics, and industrial automation.</p></p>
