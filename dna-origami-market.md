<p><p>DNA origami is a groundbreaking technique in nanotechnology that involves the self-assembly of DNA into intricate structures and shapes. This method uses a long single strand of DNA, which is folded into desired shapes by the addition of shorter 'staple' strands that bind to specific regions of the long strand, enabling the formation of complex geometries at the nanoscale. Applications of DNA origami span fields such as drug delivery, biosensing, and nanomedicine, making it a key area of interest in both research and commercial sectors.</p><p>The DNA Origami Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.4% during the forecast period, driven by increasing applications in biomedical research, diagnostics, and targeted therapy.
